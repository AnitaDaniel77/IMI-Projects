Lesson 4 — What is UX?

UX means User Experience. It includes everything related to all the interactions a user has with a company, its services and products — before, during and after using them.

Real life example

Think about ordering from Takealot. UX covers: finding the product easily, reading clear descriptions, a smooth checkout, getting a delivery notification, and whether the packaging made the product easy to open when it arrived. Every touchpoint is part of the experience.

Don Norman — who coined the term UX

Don Norman, one of the most famous researchers in design, coined the term UX. He was concerned with the full customer experience — how users find a product in store, get it home, open the box, read assembly instructions, and whether it felt intuitive and enjoyable to use. UX is about all of that.

What UX solves

Bad UX problems you've probably experienced:

  • An appliance where you can't tell if it's on or off
  • An app where finding what you want takes too long
  • A button that does nothing when you tap it — no feedback, no response

Good UX fixes this by making sure:

  • Features are discoverable — users can find what they're looking for
  • The product gives feedback on actions — users know something happened
  • Design is for the user, not what you assume the user wants

The UX process — overview

UX has been refined into an iterative process. You may need to go back to previous steps multiple times to adjust your design to suit both customer and business goals.

  • Observe — watch people doing the task
  • Identify — spot the problems they have completing it
  • Ideate — come up with ideas to address those problems
  • Prototype — build your intended solution
  • Test — test it with real users

Repeat each stage until the design is usable and enjoyable for everyone.

Key takeaway

UX is not just how something looks. It is how something works, how it feels, and whether users can achieve their goals without frustration. Designing for the user — not your assumptions about the user — is the foundation of good UX.
