Lesson 7 — The UX Process: 5 Stages

UX is a process-driven discipline. No one-size-fits-all approach exists, but following these five key stages helps ensure a successful user experience design. The process is iterative — you will go back and refine previous stages multiple times.

Stage 1 — Empathize

Understand your users' needs. Don't assume — go and listen.

  • Interview and observe real users completing tasks on the site
  • Listen to their frustrations
  • Create a persona from your research
  • Build an empathy map, a scenario and a journey map to stay grounded

Little Lemon example: watch customers try to order a meal for delivery on the website. Where do they get stuck?

Stage 2 — Define

Collate everything from your research. Identify and prioritise the key problems and pain points by importance.

  • You now know who your users are and what frustrates them
  • Write a user needs statement that clearly outlines what needs to be solved

Stage 3 — Ideate

Generate ideas. Keep an open mind — don't commit to one idea too early.

  • Sketch, brainstorm, mind map, handwrite notes
  • Develop your sketches into wireframes

A wireframe is a two-dimensional representation of the UI concerned with:

  • Placement and hierarchy of content
  • Functionalities offered
  • Expected actions users will take

Stage 4 — Prototype

A prototype is a simulation of the final product. Take your wireframe and flesh it out — add colour, buttons, text and make it interactive.

  • Simulate a real scenario for users to achieve their goals
  • You cannot assume it will work for everyone — test before releasing
  • Expect to iterate here too based on feedback

Stage 5 — Test

Present your prototype to real users and gather feedback.

  • Write a test script with clear instructions focused on completing a task
  • Participants interact with the prototype while trying to achieve the task
  • Any frustrations are captured and addressed before moving forward

Final stage — Build

Once you have listened, empathised, iterated and tested, it is time to build. Following all previous steps helps ensure the final product addresses real user needs and delivers a great experience.

Real life example

Think about the Capitec app blank screen issue. A proper UX process would have caught that in the test stage — a user trying to check their balance hits a blank screen, flags it, and the team fixes the missing error state before the update goes live.

Key takeaway

Empathize → Define → Ideate → Prototype → Test → Build. Each stage feeds the next and you can loop back at any point. The goal is a product that solves real problems for real users.
