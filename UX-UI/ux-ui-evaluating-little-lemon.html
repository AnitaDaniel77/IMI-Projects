Lesson 18 — Evaluating the Little Lemon UI

Tilly is hungry and wants to order food for delivery from her phone. What follows is almost every design principle covered so far getting violated in one flow.

What Tilly experienced

  • Tapped "Order online" and landed on a reserve a table page instead
  • Couldn't find the menu — only saw "this week's specials" and a Greek salad
  • Didn't realise "Menu" or the Greek salad image were tappable — no visual indication
  • A "Go" button with no explanation of what it does or where it leads
  • Only two menu items visible — bruschetta text was mostly hidden off-screen
  • No basket, no navigation, no way to go back
  • Two buttons ("Go" and "Add") with inconsistent labels and no clear difference in action
  • No "Add" button on the bruschetta at all
  • Testimonials appeared mid-flow for no clear reason

Principles violated

Dieter Rams

  • Principle 2 — good design makes a product useful: the ordering flow doesn't let Tilly order
  • Principle 3 — good design is aesthetic: text is hard to read, layout is cluttered
  • Principle 4 — good design makes a product understandable: nothing communicates what is tappable or what any action will do
  • Principle 8 — good design is thorough down to the last detail: missing basket, hidden content, inconsistent buttons

Nielsen's Heuristics

  • Heuristic 1 — visibility of system status: no feedback on where Tilly is in the flow or whether anything was added
  • Heuristic 4 — consistency and standards: "Go" and "Add" mean different things with no visual distinction
  • Heuristic 6 — recognition rather than recall: tappable elements are invisible, forcing Tilly to guess
  • Heuristic 8 — aesthetic and minimalist design: irrelevant content like testimonials and a reserve a table button appear mid-flow

Shneiderman's Golden Rules

  • Rule 1 — strive for consistency: button labels and designs are inconsistent across items
  • Rule 7 — support internal locus of control: Tilly has no way to go back or feel in control of the flow
  • Rule 8 — reduce short-term memory load: nothing is clearly labelled, forcing her to remember and guess at every step

The question to keep asking

What would Tilly want? Use that as your filter when redesigning these screens. Every element that doesn't help her place an order should be removed or moved.
