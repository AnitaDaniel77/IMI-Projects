Lesson 43 — Micro-interactions and Micro-animations

Micro-interactions begin with a trigger and progress through a set of instructions represented visually through animations. The animation is the visual output of the micro-interaction. They are individual product moments designed to accomplish a single task while improving the natural product flow.

Benefits of micro-interactions

  • Provide feedback on the outcome of an action
  • Complete a small task
  • Increase the feeling of direct manipulation
  • Help users see the result of their actions
  • Prevent user errors

Real life examples

Facebook Like button — hold or hover to see all reactions, tap to post one. The hamburger menu — tapping shows or hides navigation. Pull-to-refresh — dragging a list down retrieves new data. Volume control. Autocomplete in search.

Dan Saffer's 4 components of micro-interactions

1. Triggers

Manual triggers — the user interacts with the system, e.g. sliding to unlock a phone. System triggers — the system meets a condition automatically, e.g. receiving a notification.

2. Rules

Rules tell the app what to do after a trigger fires. They control how the micro-interaction is handled.

3. Feedback

Notifies the user of the trigger's response. Two questions to answer: what information does the user need, and how often does it need to happen?

4. Modes and Loops

The meta-rules of a micro-interaction. Modes are used when there is a specific demand. Loops determine how long the micro-interaction runs and whether it repeats.

Micro-animations

Simple interfaces — users see something happening directly, giving a sense of direct manipulation.

Context and change — on small screens some content is hidden. Micro- animations help users navigate and discover that hidden content.

User concentration — an animated button draws the eye and prompts action. Too much animation flattens the hierarchy and weakens the message. Use sparingly.

Rewards — a small animation after completing a form submission confirms success and delights the user. Keep it small — not a cartoon.

Key takeaway

Micro-interactions are the difference between a functional app and one that feels intuitive and delightful. They explain a lot without a single word. Use them to confirm actions, guide attention and reward completion — but only where they add value, not everywhere.
